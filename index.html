Project Scope

Literature Survey

The neurodevelopmental disorder Attention Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) affects a large percentage of children globally. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) estimate that 9.4% of children in the United States between the ages of 4 and 17 have ADHD. Inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity are hallmarks of ADHD, which can impair academic, social, and emotional functioning.Over the years, various approaches have been developed to diagnose and treat ADHD in children, including the use of technology.

EEG can be a viable diagnostic tool for ADHD, according to a study by Arns and colleagues (2012), provided multivariate analysis are carried out to capture additional sources of variability in ADHD. In order to help people with ADHD, to increase their attention span, several video games have been created, such as FOCUS, which employs brain-computer interface (BCI) and requires the EMOTIV EPOC+ equipment to play. The aim of the game is to test players' concentration and mental agility while they control an avatar. Additionally, according to study, people with ADHD show under-activation in the EEG during alertness, with variations depending on subtype. For specific subgroups of children with ADHD, neurofeedback (NF) training methods focusing on theta activity and theta/beta ratio have been offered as a solution.

Despite the availability of various technology-based tools for ADHD assessment and management, there is still a need for accessible, reliable, and user-friendly tools that can be used across different platforms. This need has led to the development of cross-platform applications that can run on different operating systems and devices. The development of a cross-platform application for ADHD assessment and management would have several advantages, including wider accessibility and convenience for users. Therefore, the purpose of this study is to develop a cross platform application “NeuroAssist ADHD Analyzer” for identifying ADHD levels in children. The application will incorporate various features, including symptom assessment, cognitive testing, and behavioral tracking, and will be designed to provide reliable and user-friendly ADHD assessment and management. By developing a cross-platform application for ADHD assessment and management, this study aims to provide a valuable tool for parents, clinicians, and educators to identify and manage ADHD symptoms in children.

Project Scope

Research Gap

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a common neurodevelopmental disorder that affects both children and adults, and its diagnosis is based on clinical criteria, which can be subjective and often leads to misdiagnosis. Therefore, there is a need for objective tools that can aid in the accurate diagnosis of ADHD. In recent years, there has been growing interest in the use of ADHD analyzing systems that utilize electroencephalogram (EEG) signals and machine learning techniques to assist in the diagnosis of ADHD. These systems provide a non-invasive and objective tool for clinicians to assist in the diagnosis of ADHD, which can lead to more accurate and timely interventions. ADHD analyzing systems can identify patterns in EEG signals that are associated with ADHD, and the machine learning algorithms used in these systems have shown promising results in achieving high accuracy in identifying ADHD subjects. The various feature extraction techniques used in these systems have been effectively used to capture the underlying neurophysiological patterns associated with ADHD.

Project Scope

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

This research problem was brought to attention when the use of medication or therapy achieves for ADHD children an efficiency of 70% to 80% with the disadvantages of side effects. The current diagnosis of ADHD in children is subjective and different according to the methodology and opinion of the specialist. It usually involves interviewing children, parents, teachers, and school staff, etc. Then the specialists make decisions based on the analyzed data and their findings. These costly therapy sessions have led to the need for inexpensive, efficient solution. To overcome the problems associated with current assessment methods and as an alternative to these problems, neurofeedback techniques have been developed to respond to these issues. But the typical existing neurofeedback systems are imperfect since they use software exercises to assess ADHD and train ADHD subjects. Therefore, the need for a more accurate system to measure real-time activity and aims to teach the user to regulate their own brain waves and also changing the neural mechanisms of cognition behavior, using electroencephalogram (EEG) was apparent. The main problem we identified is the absence of detecting accurate brain signals of brain activities, identifying the accurate level of ADHD, and developing attention and memory skills to some level in ADHD subjects. Although the Brain-Computer-Interface (BCI) system utilizes neurofeedback by accessing and processing signals in the brain, it is rarely used in ADHD treatments.

Solution

NEUROASSIST offers a valuable tool for detecting the ability to focus in children with ADHD. This system encompasses four distinct mechanisms, presenting a superior alternative to the conventional approach involving parents, teachers, and interviews with ADHD-affected children to assess the severity of ADHD. Employing a range of machine learning technologies, tools, and methodologies, this solution is well-equipped to identify and analyze the current level of ADHD, as well as measure patient progress. Its comprehensive utility caters to diverse stakeholders and anticipates future advancements.The emotiv-insight brainwear device, equipped with five channels, stands out for its developer-friendly nature and reasonably high quality. Emotiv products feature an API and comprehensive documentation, enabling seamless integration and future command execution. The key components of the emotiv-insight brainwear device include electrodes, an amplifier, a computer control module, a display, and interconnecting wires. Electrodes detect synaptic signals and transmit the data through channels, where the number of channels corresponds to the number of electrodes placed at different locations on the scalp. To overcome the potential weakness of signals emanating from the scalp, an amplifier is employed to enhance the detectability of the signals for processing and display on a computer. The processed data is then transmitted via Bluetooth to the computer for further analysis. Web Sockets facilitate the real-time display of the data on a monitor during development. As a crucial component of the system, two gaming activities will be developed for different age groups to evaluate patient progress and improvement. During gameplay, we will capture the brain signals (EEG) using the emotiv-insight brainwear device and subject them to analysis. This analysis will allow us to gauge the patient's improvement and make informed decisions regarding suitable treatments. Collectively, the subsystems integrated within our system enable a direct comparison between the brain waves of ADHD-affected children and those of typically developing children, yielding highly accurate results. This comprehensive approach offers the best solution for the aforementioned problem and serves as a valuable tool to assist doctors in providing appropriate treatments.

Project Scope

Research Objective

Develop a desktop application that can diagnose children with ADHD and determine the stages.

The desktop application is based on advanced algorithms and diagnostic tools that evaluates the symptoms of ADHD in children. These algorithms analyse the child's behavior patterns, attention span, impulsivity, and hyperactivity levels, among other parameters, to identify any signs of ADHD.

Analyze the ADHD level using EEG.

By analyzing EEG signals, these signal patterns of brain activity can be identified, and used to classify the specific type of ADHD that the child has.

Measure the Attention Level of the Child.

The goal of the game app is to assess the level of attention in children diagnosed with attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) by analyzing their EEG signals. The game was designed to be played on a computer, and it uses EEG signals to measure the concentration level of the children while they are playing the game.

Measure the Memorization Level of the child.

The goal was to develop a gamified desktop application that can recognize and analyze the level of ADHD in children. The application uses games to measure the child's attention and memory levels and provide an accurate assessment of their ADHD stage by analyzing their EEG signals.

Monitoring the progress of the child's recovery according to the current treatment

According to this game, the distance the object moves increases the child's attention. Therefore, when the distance this object moves increases, the doctor can draw some conclusions about the progress of the child's recovery.

Project Scope

Methodology

proposed system "NEUROASSIST ADHD Analyzer" contains 5 components.

1. Memorization Level Focus Game: Path-Finder Game : A level-based approach is used in this technique to provide memory and attention tasks to children to capture their brain signals during the sessions and analyze their ADHD level.

2. Attention Level Focus Game: Run Game : This computer game was developed to measure a child's ability to maintain attention on a specific task. The game is played by the child while obtaining EEG signals and facial expressions.

3. Learning Abilities Focus Game: Learning Activities : Three computer-based learning activities have been created to tackle the learning difficulties faced by children with ADHD. These activities include math, English vocabulary, and painting related activities. At the end of these activities, the child's ADHD level can be identified by analyzing the brain signals and facial expressions obtained during the activities. Both CNN and ANN models were used in this component.

4. Behavior Level Focus Activities: Hands on Activities : This component was designed to observe EEG signals during hands-on activities. EEG signals are captured while children with ADHD and those without ADHD engaging in hands-on activities such as craft projects, paper cutting, and clay modeling.

5. BOT Application for SNAP IV questionnaire The development of a bot application aimed to analyze children's ADHD levels by displaying the SNAP-IV questionnaire to parents/teachers through the app and collecting their answers.
